Water Front

Vancouver’s Waterfront Park is a 35-acer swath of prime riverfront real estate that has passed through many hands throughout Vancouver’s history. It has been home to shipyards, a lumber mill and most recently Boise Cascade Paper Mill that closed in 2006. The land, for more than a century had been inaccessible to the public. In 2014 the city dug under existing railroad tracks, at the cost of $45 million dollars. This paved the way for the waterfront project. Many of downtowns local shops as well as Esther Short Park and the Saturday Market have become destinations for both visitors and residents. The new public access to the waterfront is a short 5-minute walk to the heart of downtown Vancouver, and a 10-minute drive to downtown Portland.

For more information check out thewaterfrontvancouverusa.com

The $1.5 billion-dollar development will boost Vancouver’s economy by, creating jobs, providing housing and attracting visitors. Phase 1 began construction November of 2015 and is scheduled for completion this summer (2018). The $250 million phase offers something for everyone.

Block 4

Block 4 will be Hotel Indigo. The hotel will have 138 rooms and 40 condominiums and three restaurants that include a sky bar on the top floor.

Blocks 6 and 8

These blocks will be occupied by 263 apartments. The apartment building will also furnish office space such as Sotheby’s Real Estate and retail space like MidiCi The Neapolitan Pizza Company and more.

Block 9

This block is on the waterfront overlooking the beautiful Grant Street Pier and will provide space for two dining establishments. The WildFin American Grill is slotted to be one of the restaurants.

Block 12

Block 12 is located next to Block 9 on the waterfront and will house four restaurants. Twigs Bistro and Martini Bar and Ghost Runners Brewery are two of the establishments.

Parks
